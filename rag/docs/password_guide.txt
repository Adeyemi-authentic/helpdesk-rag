Password and Account Access Guide

Your company account uses single sign-on, which means one username and password gives you access to email, the VPN, and most internal systems. Because that one password unlocks everything, keep it strong and never share it, even with IT staff who will never ask you for it.

Resetting a forgotten password

If you cannot remember your password, go to the self-service portal at reset.company.com and choose Forgotten Password. Enter your work email and approve the verification request in the Microsoft Authenticator app. You will then be allowed to set a new password. Passwords must be at least twelve characters and include a number and a symbol.

Unlocking a locked account

After five failed sign-in attempts your account is locked for fifteen minutes as a security measure. You can wait for the lock to clear on its own, or call the service desk on extension 4357 to have it unlocked immediately. A locked account is not the same as an expired password, so check which message you are seeing before raising a ticket.

Password expiry

Company passwords expire every ninety days. You will receive email reminders starting ten days before expiry. If your password expires before you change it, you will be prompted to set a new one the next time you sign in, as long as you still have access to your second factor.

Getting more help

If you cannot complete a reset because you have lost access to your phone and Authenticator, raise a ticket in the IT portal under the Account Access category, or call the 24-hour service desk on extension 4357.
