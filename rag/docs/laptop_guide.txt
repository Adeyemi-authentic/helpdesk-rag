Laptop and Hardware Guide

Every member of staff is issued a company laptop during onboarding. The laptop remains company property and must be returned when you leave. Keep it updated and encrypted, and never leave it unattended in a public place such as a car or cafe.

Battery and charging

A healthy laptop battery lasts a full working day. If your battery drains within an hour or will not charge past a certain percentage, it may need replacing. First try a different charger and a different power outlet to rule those out, then raise a ticket so facilities can test or swap the battery.

Slow performance

If your laptop becomes slow, the most common causes are too many browser tabs and pending updates. Restart the machine at least once a week, as many staff never shut down and updates pile up. If it is still slow after a restart and closing unused apps, raise a ticket and include how long the slowness has lasted and what you were doing.

Reporting a lost or stolen laptop

A lost or stolen laptop is a security incident, not just a hardware problem. Report it immediately by calling the 24-hour service desk on extension 4357, even outside business hours, so the device can be remotely locked and wiped. Then raise a ticket so a replacement can be arranged. Because the disk is encrypted, your data cannot be read by whoever finds it.

Requesting accessories

Monitors, docking stations, keyboards, and headsets are available on request. Raise a ticket in the IT portal under the Hardware category. Standard accessories are usually delivered to your desk within two business days; anything non-standard may need manager approval first.
